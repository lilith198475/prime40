What to do after accident?

1. Call the police if:

  • Someone is hurt/injured;
  • You think any other driver may be guilty of a Criminal Code offence, such as drunk driving;
  • You suspect you are the victim of a staged collision;
  • There is a significant property damage or the vehicle is not driveable.

2. If it is a minor accident, no one is injured and total damage to all the vehicles involved appears to be LESSS than $1,000, call your local policy for instructions.

3. If the local police do not attend the scene of the accident, they will instruct you to report to a Collision Reporting Centre within 24 hours.

4. Write down other driver’s information such as name, address, driver’s license number, name of insurance company and policy number.

5. Also obtain the names, addresses, and telephone numbers of passengers and witnesses.

6. Jot down specific details about the scene of the accident.

7. Report the accident to your insurance company or broker.
